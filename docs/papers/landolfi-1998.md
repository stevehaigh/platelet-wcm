# Landolfi et al. (1998) — Mitochondria–ER functional interactions in the agonist-sensitive store

- **Full citation:** Landolfi B, Curci S, Debellis L, Pozzan T & Hofer AM. "Ca²⁺ Homeostasis in the Agonist-sensitive Internal Store: Functional Interactions Between Mitochondria and the ER Measured In Situ in Intact Cells." *Journal of Cell Biology* 142(5):1235–1243, 1998.
- **Type:** experimental (in situ intraluminal [Ca²⁺] fluorescence imaging)
- **Local PDF:** source-info/calcium-papers/Landolfi et al. - 1998 - Ca2+ Homeostasis in the Agonist-sensitive Internal Store Functional Interactions Between Mitochondr.pdf
- **Used in the model for:** background/justification for the mitochondrial Ca²⁺ buffer coupling to store refilling (MCU term and store dynamics).

## What the paper does
Using low-affinity trapped fluorescent indicators (mag-fura-2) to read intraluminal ER [Ca²⁺] directly in single intact and permeabilised BHK-21 cells, the authors test how mitochondrial Ca²⁺ buffering and mitochondrial ATP production shape ER store refilling after agonist (bradykinin)-induced release.

## Key findings / values the model uses
- Mitochondrial inhibitors (FCCP, antimycin A, oligomycin, rotenone) **dramatically and persistently prolonged the refilling** of internal stores after bradykinin stimulation (Figs. 1, 3).
- This was largely due to inhibition of Ca²⁺ entry at the plasma membrane, but a significant component arose from **reduced SERCA-mediated Ca²⁺ uptake**, attributed to ATP depletion in a localised subcellular (microdomain) compartment.
- The rate of bradykinin-induced Ca²⁺ release was **reduced to 51% of control** by FCCP; this effect was largely overcome by loading cells with BAPTA-AM — highlighting that mitochondrial Ca²⁺ buffering shapes the release kinetics.
- Direct refilling rates after store inhibition were on average only **18.3 ± 2.5% of control** values in the same cell (n = 23).
- Conclusion: competent, actively respiring mitochondria are essential for generating "explosive" Ca²⁺ signals; mitochondria and ER are intimately (and possibly physically) coupled via local Ca²⁺ buffering and ATP-producing microdomains.

## Relevance to the platelet WCM
Supports treating mitochondria as a cytosolic Ca²⁺ buffer/sink (the MCU buffer term) that interacts with store dynamics, and supports the bioenergetic argument that mitochondrial ATP feeds SERCA refilling — the design rationale behind the proposed MCU→ATP→SOCE coupling (#76 Part 2).

## Caveats / notes
BHK-21 cells (ER), not platelets (DTS); the store is the classical ER. The "reduced release on mitochondrial inhibition" finding is consistent in direction with the experimental MCU-loss-reduces-signal papers (Ghatge 2026, Ajanel 2025) and therefore points the same way as the project's known model-vs-experiment divergence: the WCM's buffer-only MCU does not reproduce a release/SERCA-coupling reduction.
