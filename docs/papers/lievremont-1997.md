# Lièvremont et al. (1997) — BiP / HSPA5 as an ER luminal Ca²⁺ storage protein

- **Full citation:** Lièvremont J-P, Rizzuto R, Hendershot L, Meldolesi J. "BiP, a Major Chaperone Protein of the Endoplasmic Reticulum Lumen, Plays a Direct and Important Role in the Storage of the Rapidly Exchanging Pool of Ca²⁺." *Journal of Biological Chemistry* 272(49):30873–30879, 1997.
- **Type:** experimental
- **Local PDF:** source-info/calcium-papers/Lièvremont et al. - 1997 - BiP, a Major Chaperone Protein of the Endoplasmic Reticulum Lumen, Plays a Direct and Important Role.pdf
- **Used in the model for:** Ca²⁺-buffering stoichiometry and store-fraction contribution of BiP (HSPA5) in the multi-buffer DTS luminal store (issue #25, Phase 3).

## What the paper does
HeLa cells were transiently transfected with hamster BiP (or a 44 kDa N-terminal ATPase-domain mutant, 44KΔBiP, lacking the protein-binding/chaperone domain) and assayed for ER Ca²⁺ storage by two independent techniques: cytosolic/mitochondrial aequorin (Ca²⁺ release on IP₃-agonist or ionophore stimulation) and ⁴⁵Ca equilibrium loading with thapsigargin-induced release. Parallel controls used calreticulin (CRT) and CaBP2/ERp72.

## Key findings / values the model uses
- BiP overexpression (total BiP ~200% of control) **increased the ER rapidly-exchanging Ca²⁺ store capacity**; ⁴⁵Ca/thapsigargin release was distinctly greater than controls.
- Endogenous BiP **contributes ~25% of the resting rapidly-exchanging ER Ca²⁺ store** (calculated from the ~25% increase in Tg-releasable ⁴⁵Ca at 2× BiP).
- **Stoichiometry ~1–2 moles Ca²⁺ per mole BiP**, low affinity (no canonical EF-hands; binding ascribed to acidic residue doublets/triplets).
- The chaperone-dead 44KΔBiP mutant gave the same effect → Ca²⁺ storage is direct binding, not a chaperone-mediated artefact.
- Comparators: CRT contributes ~50% of the store; CaBP2/ERp72 gave **no** detectable increase (overexpression of any luminal Ca²⁺-binding protein does not automatically raise capacity).
- Stress (tunicamycin) raised BiP (~100%) and CRT (~58%), increasing Tg-releasable Ca²⁺ by ~68%; BiP and CRT contributed ~25% and ~29% of that increase.
- BiP in HeLa is ~1% of total protein (~5× higher than CRT).

## Relevance to the platelet WCM
Provides BiP's per-molecule Ca²⁺ stoichiometry (1–2 sites, low affinity) and an empirical "≈25% of store" benchmark for apportioning the DTS luminal buffer budget among BiP, CRT, GRP94 and the CREC proteins. The CaBP2-negative result is a useful caution that buffer contribution must be measured, not assumed from EF-hand/charge presence.

## Caveats / notes
Measured in HeLa ER, not platelet DTS; the ~25% store fraction is a HeLa-specific apportionment and may differ in platelets. The Ca²⁺ affinity is described qualitatively as low; a numeric Kd is not reported (the purified protein had not given conclusive affinity data at the time).
