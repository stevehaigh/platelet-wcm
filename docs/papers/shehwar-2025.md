# Shehwar et al. (2025) — Platelets and mitochondria: the calcium connection (review)

- **Full citation:** Shehwar D, Barki S, Aliotta A, Bertaggia Calderara D, Veuthey L, Pereira Portela C, Alberio L & Alam MR. "Platelets and mitochondria: the calcium connection." *Molecular Biology Reports* 52:276, 2025. DOI: 10.1007/s11033-025-10389-3.
- **Type:** review (mini-review)
- **Local PDF:** source-info/calcium-papers/Shehwar et al. - 2025 - Platelets and mitochondria the calcium connection.pdf
- **Used in the model for:** orienting review of platelet mitochondrial Ca²⁺ machinery and its place in the wider Ca²⁺ network (MCU, DTS, SOCE, NCLX).

## What the paper does
Summarises recent developments in mitochondrial Ca²⁺ transport in platelet physiology — how agonist-induced Ca²⁺ mobilisation from the DTS, coupled with SOCE and non-SOCE (ROCE) entry, regulates platelet functions, and how mitochondria participate via the MCU complex.

## Key findings / values the model uses
- Resting platelets contain **5–8 mitochondria** distributed in the cytoplasm; activated platelets have more, smaller mitochondria from fission.
- Ca²⁺ signalling pathway recap: receptor → PLC hydrolyses PIP2 → IP3 + DAG; IP3 releases Ca²⁺ from the **DTS via IP3 receptors**; DAG drives ROCE through TRPC6 and P2X1; store depletion triggers **SOCE** via STIM1 oligomerisation and Orai1; **SERCA** refills the DTS and **PMCA + NCX** extrude Ca²⁺.
- Mitochondrial Ca²⁺ transport machinery: **VDAC** (outer membrane), the **MCU complex (MCUC)** in the inner membrane (pore = MCU with a DIME Asp-Ile-Met-Glu selectivity filter; MCUb a dominant-negative paralog); matrix Ca²⁺ efflux via the inner-membrane **Na⁺/Ca²⁺ exchanger NCLX**.
- Mitochondria buffer rising cytosolic Ca²⁺ and can store **up to ~20-fold more Ca²⁺ than basal** during platelet hyperactivation (e.g. dual-agonist stimulation), where matrix Ca²⁺ crossing a threshold opens the **mitochondrial permeability transition pore (mPTP)** → phosphatidylserine exposure / procoagulant (and apoptotic) platelets.

## Relevance to the platelet WCM
Confirms and contextualises the compartment topology the WCM uses (cytosol / DTS / mitochondria / plasmalemma / extracellular) and the role of MCU as a cytosolic Ca²⁺ buffer — the conceptual basis of the model's buffer-style MCU term — while flagging the downstream mPTP/procoagulant branch the single-cell model does not represent.

## Caveats / notes
A review, not a source of fitted rate constants. It frames mitochondrial Ca²⁺ uptake as buffering (consistent with the WCM term) but also describes downstream consequences (mPTP, PS exposure, NCLX efflux) outside the current model scope. Useful as a literature map rather than a parameter source.
