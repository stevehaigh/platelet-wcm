# Vorum et al. (1998) — Molecular cloning of human calumenin and its Ca²⁺-binding activity

- **Full citation:** Vorum H, Liu X, Madsen P, Rasmussen HH, Honoré B. *Molecular cloning of a cDNA encoding human calumenin, expression in Escherichia coli and analysis of its Ca²⁺-binding activity.* Biochim. Biophys. Acta 1386:121–131, 1998.
- **Type:** experimental (molecular cloning + Ca²⁺-binding characterisation)
- **Local PDF:** source-info/calcium-papers/Vorum et al. - 1998 - Molecular cloning of a cDNA encoding human calumenin, expression in Escherichia coli and anal.pdf
- **Used in the model for:** characterising calumenin (CALU) as a low-affinity, high-capacity luminal Ca²⁺ buffer in the DTS/ER store.

## What the paper does
Identifies the transformation-sensitive protein spot IEF SSP 9302 as the human homologue of calumenin, clones its cDNA, expresses the recombinant protein in *E. coli*, and measures its Ca²⁺-binding stoichiometry and affinity by equilibrium microdialysis (Scatchard analysis).

## Key findings / values the model uses
- Calumenin is a **315-amino-acid** protein with a 19-aa N-terminal signal sequence, **7 EF-hand domains**, and a C-terminal **HDEF** ER-retrieval signal (predicted MW 37.1 kDa precursor / 35.1 kDa mature; pI ~4.3; observed ~48.9 kDa on 2-D gel).
- It belongs to the **CREC family** of multiple-EF-hand secretory-pathway proteins (with reticulocalbin/ERC-55/Cab45).
- **All 7 EF-hands bind Ca²⁺**, each with a **low intrinsic affinity of ~1.6 × 10³ M⁻¹** (i.e. Kd in the high-µM/mM range), measured by ⁴⁵Ca²⁺ microdialysis.
- Ubiquitously expressed; highest in heart, placenta, skeletal muscle.

## Relevance to the platelet WCM
One of the luminal-buffer sources behind the **multi-buffer DTS store** (issue #25): the DTS does not hold free Ca²⁺ alone but a large buffered pool. Calumenin's low-affinity/high-capacity profile (7 sites × many copies) is the kind of buffering that lets the store release substantial Ca²⁺ while its *free* concentration stays in the measured 100–400 µM band. Companion buffer sources: GRP94 ([argon-simen-1999](argon-simen-1999.md)), BiP ([lievremont-1997](lievremont-1997.md)), and the CREC-family review ([honore-vorum-2000](honore-vorum-2000.md)).

## Caveats / notes
The very low EF-hand affinity (~10³ M⁻¹) means calumenin contributes capacity, not high-affinity buffering; the model lumps luminal buffers rather than representing each protein's sites individually. Exact platelet copy number of calumenin is taken from proteome sources, not this paper.
