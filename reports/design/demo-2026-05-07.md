## What the model is (30 seconds)

- Single-cell platelet whole-cell model, 1-second discrete timesteps,
  built on the CovertLab/wcEcoli simulation framework (process / state
  / listener / ODE pattern reused; all E. coli biology removed and
  replaced).
- **27 ODE state species**: IP3R 6-state Markov (Sneyd & Dufour 2002),
  SERCA 6-state E1/E2 cycle (Dode 2002 / Purvis 2008), PMCA 5-state
  CaM-coupled (Caride 2007 Table 3), CaM Ca²⁺-binding ladder,
  STIM/Orai MWC SOCE (Hoover & Lewis 2011 / Dolan 2014), IP3 forcing
  curve (Dolan Fig. S2).
- **Validation target**: Dolan & Diamond 2014 Biophys J — the most-cited
  platelet Ca²⁺ model.

## The honest limitation (one paragraph)

The DTS empties to 0 within ~5 s under stimulation in either condition
because IP3R inflow at the published 10 pS conductance (~112 k ions/s
at full DTS) overwhelms SERCA's max forward delivery (~8 k ions/s,
hard-capped by E2P·Ca occupancy upstream of the release step). Once the
DTS is empty, IP3R has nothing left to release; the SOCE-driven plateau
that Dolan's Fig. 4 shows in the +Ca_ex condition cannot form because
the cytosolic Ca²⁺ has already collapsed by the time SOCE engages
(STIM1 dimers do rise from 22 to ~810 — the store-sensing mechanism is
working, but its product comes too late).

This is documented in **design doc §6.8 D7** and in the **2026-05-06
lab books** (path B falsification + Phase 3 results).

## Likely Q&A

**Q: Why don't the two conditions give different peaks?**
A: The peak is set by IP3R drainage of the DTS, which finishes in ~5 s
in both cases. SOCE (the only mechanism that *would* differ between
conditions) acts after that, but cyt Ca²⁺ has already collapsed by
then. The store-sensing machinery is working (STIM1 dimers rise from
22 to ~810 in the +Ca_ex case); SOCE simply engages too late to
contribute to the peak.

**Q: Why does the resting / no-stimulus run still spike?**
A: Same Phase 2 issue, made cleanly visible. The Dolan Table S1
initial conditions aren't a steady state of our ODE — at t=0 the IP3R
sub-states give Po⁴ ≈ 1.65 × 10⁻⁵, which combined with γ_IP3R = 10 pS
and a full DTS produces ~112 000 ions/s of cyt Ca²⁺ inflow even at the
50 nM IP3 baseline (no stimulus required). The system rushes from the
initial conditions toward its actual fixed point at ~3 nM cyt within
~15 s; from there the trace is flat — *that's* the real resting
behaviour, just at the wrong concentration. Documented in design doc
§6.8 D7; issue #19 tracks the work to re-derive initial conditions
that put the fixed point at 100 nM. The spike isn't biology, it's an
initial-conditions artefact, and showing it is part of the honest
story.

**Q: Why not just tune the model?**
A: We tried twice. **Phase 2a (2026-05-05)** swept γ_IP3R across
8 orders of magnitude — at any γ low enough to balance SERCA, the
system gives DTS *overshoot* (~450 µM) and cyt *undershoot* (~3 nM):
opposite imbalance, same magnitude problem. **Path B (2026-05-06)**
swept SERCA `k_release_r` across 8 orders of magnitude — also
falsified, because the cycle's forward throughput is hard-capped at
~8 000 ions/s by E2P·Ca occupancy *upstream* of the release step;
`k_release_r` controls only the reverse leak, not the ceiling. The
real fix needs either multi-parameter calibration (low-confidence,
hard to defend in writing) or a missing biology compartment (better
story).

**Q: What's the next step then?**
A: Two natural directions, captured as separate issues:

- **#19** — re-derive the resting initial conditions from the
  converged ODE. Once we accept that our cycle's fixed point isn't
  Dolan's, finding it numerically (run un-stimulated for 300 s, take
  the converged state as the new initial counts) is the cleanest move
  for fixing the resting spike and getting a defensible at-rest state.
- **#22** — add the mitochondrial Ca²⁺ uniporter (MCU). Three
  platelet MCU papers in `source-info/calcium-papers/` (Ajanel 2025,
  Ghatge 2026, Shehwar 2025) — current research direction, missing
  from Dolan 2014. Estimate 1–2 days. Adds a new compartment that
  buffers Ca²⁺ during the spike and slowly releases it over minutes.

**Q: Will adding MCU fix the failing acceptance criteria?**
A: Honestly, probably not the *peak differential* one. The SOCE
differential criterion compares the peaks of the two conditions, and
the peak in our model is set by IP3R drainage in the first ~5 s —
*before* MCU dynamics or any post-peak buffer can dominate. MCU is a
post-peak buffer; it will fix the cosmetic post-spike collapse to
~3 nM (stronger biological story, citable extension to Ajanel /
Ghatge), and it will give a measurable shape difference between
conditions in the recovery phase, but it won't bend the peaks far
apart. Lifting the SOCE peak differential criterion needs the
upstream initial-conditions / cycle-throughput work in #19.

**Q: How does this compare to Dolan?**
A: Dolan published their model with a homeostatic-filtered population
(0.06 % of 2.6 M sampled initial conditions). Our v0.2 implements the
same equations with the same primary-source rate constants, but the
published Dolan initial conditions are not a steady state of our ODE
because of cycle-throughput arithmetic that didn't matter for Dolan's
filtering work — they sampled across initial conditions and rate
parameters jointly, then filtered. The deviation is fully traced and
documented as a v0.2 known limitation in design doc §6.8.

**Q: Is this dissertation-grade?**
A: The peak amplitude criterion (the *primary* biological-validity
result Dolan uses) is met in both ±extracellular-Ca²⁺ conditions.
The SOCE-shape criterion isn't, and that's honest framing for a
Methods/Results section: *"v0.2 reproduces Dolan's peak amplitude
criterion; the SOCE-dependent plateau distinguishing the conditions
is identified as requiring a mitochondrial buffer (#22) plus a
re-derived resting initial conditions vector (#19)."*

## Commands cheat sheet

```bash
# Webapp (local)
make run                          # http://localhost:8050/
make stop

# Phase 3 reproducer (both ±Ca²⁺ conditions + comparison plot, ~60 s)
PYTHONPATH=$PWD OPENBLAS_NUM_THREADS=1 MPLBACKEND=Agg \
    pyenv exec python runscripts/manual/runPhase3.py phase3_demo --length 200

# Single sim — three biologically-distinct conditions, exposed via flags:

# (1) IP3 transient with extracellular Ca²⁺ (Phase 1, default)
PYTHONPATH=$PWD OPENBLAS_NUM_THREADS=1 \
    pyenv exec python runscripts/manual/runPlateletSim.py transient --length 200

# (2) EDTA transient (Phase 3 no-extracellular-Ca²⁺)
PYTHONPATH=$PWD OPENBLAS_NUM_THREADS=1 \
    pyenv exec python runscripts/manual/runPlateletSim.py edta --length 200 --ca-ex-mM 0

# (3) Resting (no IP3 stimulus)
PYTHONPATH=$PWD OPENBLAS_NUM_THREADS=1 \
    pyenv exec python runscripts/manual/runPlateletSim.py \
        resting --length 300 --no-ip3-forcing

# Tests
PYTHONPATH=$PWD OPENBLAS_NUM_THREADS=1 MPLBACKEND=Agg \
    pyenv exec python -m pytest models/platelet/tests/ -q

# Type check
PYTHONPATH=$PWD pyenv exec python -m mypy models/platelet/ reconstruction/platelet/
```
